# Career Portfolio: Engineering

## Tier Goals

**Tier 1: Elite Motorsport (North Star Dream)**
* **Goal:** Become a Formula 1 engineer.
* **Motivation:** Formula 1 is the ultimate arena for applied engineering — aerodynamics, power units, materials, and vehicle dynamics pushed to their limits.
* **Significance:** Symbolizes mastery of mechanical engineering at the highest performance frontier.

**Tier 2: Adjacent Motorsport**
* **Goal:** Work in other elite racing categories such as Formula E, WEC, MotoGP, or IndyCar.
* **Motivation:** These environments demand similar technical intensity and provide fulfilling careers that parallel F1 in challenge and prestige.

**Tier 3: High-Performance Automotive & Aerospace Engineering**
* **Goal:** Contribute to R&D in companies like Ferrari’s road cars division, Tesla, Porsche, Airbus, Rolls-Royce, or SpaceX.
* **Motivation:** These fields balance excitement and innovation with more stability than motorsport. They represent a practical extension of the same engineering passions (aero, power, materials, performance).

**Tier 4: Mobility & Transportation Systems**
* **Goal:** Work in broader mobility/transportation engineering — EVs, sustainable mass transit, autonomous vehicles, or rail systems.
* **Motivation:** Strong societal impact and financial security, while staying tied to the vehicle/mechanical domain. Represents a grounded fallback option, ensuring stability without losing connection to engineering.

---

## The 11 Pillars

1. Solidify core engineering foundations
2. Develop specialized domain knowledge
3. Master key technical skills and tools
4. Gain practical experience through projects and internships
5. Expand knowledge to adjacent technologies
6. Build a network and professional profile
7. Prepare strategically for opportunities
8. Portfolio & public proof (visible artifacts)
9. Feedback loops & mentors (regular reviews)
10. Credentials & logistics (GPA, exams, scholarships, visas, timelines)
11. Sustainability (health, stress management, consistent cadence)
